What is The Traveling Therapist?

The traveling therapist is a matchmaking service operating via auction. The traveling therapist connects clinics looking for temporary coverage for their therapists on vacation, leave, etc. and (physio)therapists looking for temporary, casual, or extra work in new clinical environments. Clinics retain their level of client care and patient load while their regular staff are away, while therapists get paid to work in new environments with new clinical populations and network in the process.

How do auctions work?

Auction work via reverse bidding. The clinic posts an opening to which therapists post their bid to complete the entire work term listed. This bid is how much they are willing to accept as payment for the entire term of the ad posted. Therapists may continue to offer their services for lower and lower prices if they are willing to complete the term for that price. This process continues until the auction timer runs out, at which time, the traveling therapist matches the lowest bidding therapist with the clinic in need.

Frequently Asked Questions

The traveling therapist matches the lowest bidding therapist and the clinic within 48h of a match via email. This is the handoff process, from which point finances are handled outside of the traveling therapist. Note that therapists attempting to contact the clinic directly can be banned from the service as outlined in the terms & conditions.

Upon an auction ending, the traveling therapist charges the clinic's credit card on file in the amount of 5%+HST or $225+HST based on the final auction value, whichever is larger.

Clinics post ads for free. Therapists bid for free. The traveling therapist charges a 5% fee based on the lowest bidder contract value of any completed auction. We charge a maximum of $5,000+HST per transaction for large auctions and $225+HST minimum cost.

We store the following information, privately and encrypted on our end. We only share therapist contact information with the clinic if you're the lowest bidding therapist at the end of an auction. See full details with our privacy policy, here.
